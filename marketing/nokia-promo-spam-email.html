by SEO Mofo on Oct 30th, 2009

in Marketing

The email in this post is not really from Nokia. It is from an anonymous spammer trying to scam people. Nokia presumably has no affiliation with this email or the bogus promotion it describes. All mentions of Nokia in this post are referring to the fake spam version, not the real company that makes phones. Additionally, all names and addresses are presumed to be fictional.

The first redundancy is obvious, but there’s also this to consider: If they only selected 25,000 company e-mail addresses and 30,000,00 individual e-mail addresses for this drawing, then they would need at most (assuming Mr. Spammer lost a zero) 30,025,000 unique combinations of IDs to assign. A simple 8-digit number would suffice. Why then would Nokia assign each entrant 3 different ID numbers of 6-8 alphanumeric characters each? You could assign a unique ID to every atom in the Universe with a system like that.
